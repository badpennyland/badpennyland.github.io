Make Your Own Mini-Screen

May 16, 2017
How-To

I made my own mini-screens since I'm hard-pressed to find a ready-made screen smaller than 8"x10". I wanted to experiment with using smaller screens, almost like stamps, to fill out my larger-scale prints and come a little closer to an all-over print kind of feel. Or at least as a way to add to/modify a larger print design. I wanted to be able to still create a batch run of shirts/prints, while also being able to add or modify that design with smaller screens with smaller designs, so each shirt or print is unique despite being batch printed.


Wanna do it yourself? Here's what you will need.

THE SCREEN
I got a handful of 4"x6" wood frames from Michaels for $1 and a yard of screenprinting mesh fabric from Amazon (160 mesh count, white).

THE REST
You might have these lying around, but you will need medium sized binder clips (at least 12) and wood glue. What you might not have lying around is a small squeegee around 3" wide or so. I haven't tested this out yet, but I think something like this would work fine.

I'm still testing this out, so as I experiment I will post about any updates. If you try this yourself, let me know if you come up with any issues or better ways to make your own mini screens (comments or email).
